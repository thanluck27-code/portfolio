Case 01 · 2024

Health Wallet

A blockchain-backed personal health record that gives patients ownership of their medical data.

Role
Product Designer · UX Research
Platform
Mobile App
Timeline
6 months
Tools
Figma, Miro, Maze
01Problem

Patients across Thai hospitals had no single, trustworthy place to access their own medical history — records were fragmented, paper-bound, and rarely portable between providers.

02Research
  • Ran a 200-participant quantitative survey on Maze covering healthcare habits, data access, and tech adoption.
  • Conducted in-depth interviews with primary caregivers and chronically ill patients aged 25–54 in Bangkok.
  • Mapped the end-to-end hospital-transfer journey to identify where patient data fell out of sync.
  • Benchmarked PDPA-compliant health record products to validate consent and data-sharing patterns.
03Insight

“People don't want more health data — they want one calm place to find what matters, and the confidence to share it on their own terms.”

04Solution
/01

Designed a wallet metaphor: medical records, prescriptions, and consents live as cards a user can hand to a provider.

/02

Built a granular consent flow so patients authorize hospitals per-visit, per-record — not blanket access.

/03

Created a caregiver mode so a daughter can manage her parent's records without compromising privacy.

/04

Integrated virtual consult, e-prescription, and health education into one tab system to reduce cognitive load.

05Impact
94%
Task success in usability tests
−38%
Time to retrieve records
4.7
Avg. trust score (out of 5)

Case 02 · 2024

EVER Medical Tourism

UX audit and redesign of a one-stop medical-tourism booking platform serving global patients.

Role
Product Designer · UX Audit
Platform
Responsive Web
Timeline
3 months
Tools
Figma, Miro
01Problem

International patients booked treatment abroad with high anxiety — the existing site buried pricing, hid credentials, and made it nearly impossible to compare clinics side by side.

02Research
  • Heuristic audit of every key flow, color-coded by severity (quick-fix, needs-improvement, good-default, new-idea).
  • Competitive teardown of 8 medical-tourism and aesthetic platforms across user flow, monetization, and trust signals.
  • Stakeholder voting workshop with 5 points per participant to prioritize the most painful flows.
  • Synthesized findings into a prioritized fix list anchored to revenue and conversion impact.
03Insight

“Trust is the product. Every screen either earns it — with credentials, real prices, and reviewer faces — or quietly loses the booking.”

04Solution
/01

Restructured Home, Clinic Explore, Clinic Profile, and Treatment Explore as a single trust-building funnel.

/02

Surfaced doctor credentials, certifications, and verified reviews above the fold on every clinic profile.

/03

Designed a transparent price-comparison module so users can weigh treatment, travel, and recovery in one view.

/04

Standardized the booking pattern so the same primary action lives in the same place across all flows.

05Impact
+42%
Projected booking conversion
23
Critical UX issues resolved
4 flows
Rebuilt end-to-end

Case 03 · 2023

Hospital Information System

Enterprise HIS modules designed sprint-by-sprint for a Thai government hospital network.

Role
Product Designer · UI Systems
Platform
Desktop HIS
Timeline
1 week per module
Tools
Figma, Design Tokens
01Problem

Clinicians were losing minutes per patient navigating an inconsistent legacy HIS — every module looked, behaved, and failed differently, and engineering had no shared design language to build against.

02Research
  • Partnered with Business Analysts to translate dense clinical requirements into testable interaction specs.
  • Shadowed doctors and nurses across consulting, ordering, and antenatal flows to map real-world constraints.
  • Audited existing modules for inconsistent components, dead-end states, and missing data hierarchies.
  • Established a component contract with engineering so handoff was reusable, not re-built every sprint.
03Insight

“In a clinical setting, consistency isn't a nice-to-have — it's how you prevent the kind of error that costs a patient.”

04Solution
/01

Designed the Consulting → Iodine Clinic interface with information density tuned for fast, repeated use.

/02

Built an Order Set management module that lets clinicians create, edit, and reuse complex order bundles.

/03

Delivered an Antenatal Care & Labor Room flow covering pregnancy tracking through delivery in one record.

/04

Documented patterns into a reusable component library so engineering shipped UI consistently across sprints.

05Impact

HIS modules shipped

Case 04 · 2023

Green Cycle

An on-demand recycling pickup app turning household waste into income for time-poor city dwellers.

Role
Product Designer · End-to-End
Platform
Mobile App
Timeline
6 weeks
Tools
Figma
01Problem

COVID-era online shopping created mountains of packaging waste at home, but recycling centers required time, transport, and effort that working professionals simply didn't have.

02Research
  • Diary study with work-from-home professionals tracking waste accumulation and disposal friction over two weeks.
  • Interviewed independent recyclers and small collection businesses to understand the supply side of the market.
  • Benchmarked food-delivery and on-demand service patterns — the mental model users already trusted.
  • Validated willingness-to-pay vs. willingness-to-be-paid for different waste categories.
03Insight

“People don't refuse to recycle — they refuse to add another errand. Pickup at their door, on their schedule, flips the entire equation.”

04Solution
/01

Designed a flow where users log waste type and weight, get an instant price estimate, and book a pickup slot.

/02

Built clear visual guides for sorting and preparation, so users know exactly what to bag before pickup.

/03

Integrated cash and bank-transfer payouts on pickup day to remove friction and build immediate trust.

/04

Added a recycling impact dashboard so users see their personal carbon and waste reduction over time.

05Impact
−65%
